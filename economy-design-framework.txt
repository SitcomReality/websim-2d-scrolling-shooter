GAME ECONOMY & PROGRESSION DESIGN FRAMEWORK
===========================================

I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document establishes a comprehensive economic framework for a procedurally-generated bullet-hell roguelike where each run is self-contained with no meta-progression. The goal is to create a mathematically sound system where player power, enemy difficulty, and resource acquisition scale in harmony to maintain engagement throughout a 15-30 minute run.


II. CURRENT STATE ANALYSIS

A. IDENTIFIED PROBLEMS

1. CURRENCY MISMATCH
   - Enemies drop 1-10 currency with 50% drop chance
   - Early game: ~1 currency/second (0.5 enemies/sec × 50% drop × 2 avg currency)
   - Shop items cost 150-240 currency
   - Players need 2.5-4 MINUTES of perfect play to afford ONE item
   - This creates frustration and makes the shop feel inaccessible

2. XP SCALING DISCONNECT
   - XP requirement: 100 → 150 → 225 → 338 → 507... (×1.5 per level)
   - XP gain: 10-50 per enemy kill (static, doesn't scale)
   - Enemy spawn: 0.5/second (static)
   - Time to level scales exponentially while power gain is linear
   - Level 1→2: ~20 seconds, Level 5→6: ~100 seconds

3. ARBITRARY UPGRADE VALUES
   - Damage: +1 common, +2 uncommon, +3 rare, +5 legendary
   - No relationship to enemy HP pools or DPS requirements
   - Basic enemy: 10 HP (10 shots at +0, 5 shots at +5)
   - Tank enemy: 30 HP (30 shots at +0, 6 shots at +5)
   - Upgrades feel weak because HP pools don't account for them

4. NO PACING FRAMEWORK
   - Enemy spawn rate is constant (every 2 seconds)
   - No intensity curves or breathing room
   - No escalation tied to player power
   - No "boss moments" or milestone events

5. FRACTURED CURRENCIES
   - XP grants levels → upgrades
   - Currency buys shop items
   - No interaction between systems
   - Players optimize one path, ignore the other


III. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

A. CORE PRINCIPLES

1. TRANSPARENT POWER FANTASY
   - Players should FEEL their progression immediately
   - Every upgrade should create visible impact
   - Numbers should be intuitive (prefer 10→15 over 1.0→1.5)

2. MEANINGFUL CHOICES UNDER PRESSURE
   - Resources should be scarce enough to force decisions
   - No single dominant strategy
   - Risk/reward tradeoffs at multiple timescales

3. EMERGENT COMPLEXITY
   - Simple base rules that combine in interesting ways
   - Avoid hidden mechanics or obtuse formulas
   - Players should understand "why" without reading documentation

4. SUSTAINABLE DIFFICULTY CURVE
   - Challenge scales with player power
   - Failure should feel fair, success should feel earned
   - Difficulty spikes followed by consolidation periods


IV. UNIFIED ECONOMIC FRAMEWORK

A. SINGLE CURRENCY SYSTEM

RECOMMENDATION: Eliminate the XP/Currency split. Use a single "Energy" resource.

WHY:
- Reduces cognitive load
- Allows flexible spending strategies
- Enables meaningful economic decisions
- Simplifies balancing

MECHANICS:
- Enemies drop Energy shards on death
- Energy grants upgrades at specific thresholds
- Energy can be spent in shop OR saved for next upgrade
- Creates tension: "Do I buy this item now or save for my next upgrade?"

B. ENERGY ECONOMY

BASE FORMULA:
   EnergyToLevel(n) = 50 × (1.4)^(n-1)

   Level 1→2: 50 Energy
   Level 2→3: 70 Energy  
   Level 3→4: 98 Energy
   Level 4→5: 137 Energy
   Level 5→6: 192 Energy
   Level 10: 1,076 Energy

RATIONALE:
- Starts accessible (first level in ~20 seconds)
- 1.4x scaling is gentler than current 1.5x
- Round numbers for clarity
- Predictable without calculator

ENERGY DROP RATES:

   Enemy Type    | HP  | Energy Drop | Spawn Weight
   --------------|-----|-------------|-------------
   Basic         | 15  | 2-4         | 60%
   Fast          | 12  | 3-5         | 20%
   Zigzag        | 18  | 4-6         | 15%
   Tank          | 35  | 8-12        | 5%

DROP CHANCE: 100% (every enemy drops energy)

EARLY GAME MATH (Level 1, 0.5 enemies/sec):
   - 60% basic (3 avg) + 20% fast (4 avg) + 20% zigzag (5 avg)
   - Average: 3.6 Energy per enemy
   - Rate: 1.8 Energy/second
   - Time to Level 2: ~28 seconds
   - Breathing room for player to learn mechanics

MID GAME MATH (Level 5, 1.0 enemies/sec, tougher mix):
   - Spawn rate increased, Tank more common
   - Average: 5 Energy per enemy  
   - Rate: 5 Energy/second
   - Time to Level 6: ~38 seconds
   - Maintains pace despite harder enemies


C. ENEMY SCALING

SPAWN RATE FORMULA:
   SpawnDelay(level) = max(800, 2000 - (level × 120))

   Level 1: 2000ms (0.5/sec)
   Level 5: 1400ms (0.71/sec)
   Level 10: 800ms (1.25/sec)
   Level 15+: 800ms (capped)

ENEMY TYPE UNLOCK:
   Level 1-2: Basic only
   Level 3-4: Basic (60%) + Fast (40%)
   Level 5-7: Basic (40%) + Fast (30%) + Zigzag (30%)
   Level 8+: All types with Tank at 5-10%

HP SCALING:
   EnemyHP(level) = BaseHP × (1 + (level - 1) × 0.08)

   Basic at Level 1: 15 HP
   Basic at Level 5: 19 HP
   Basic at Level 10: 21 HP
   Tank at Level 10: 60 HP

RATIONALE:
- HP scales slower than player damage
- Player gets stronger RELATIVE to enemies
- Creates power fantasy while maintaining challenge through numbers
- 8% per level feels fair (10% would be too steep)


D. SHOP ECONOMY

ITEM COST FORMULA:
   ItemCost(tier) = EnergyToLevel(tier × 2)

   Tier 1 (Levels 1-3): 70 Energy
   Tier 2 (Levels 4-6): 137 Energy
   Tier 3 (Levels 7-10): 274 Energy
   Tier 4 (Levels 11+): 548 Energy

SHOP TIMING:
- Shop opens after every level-up
- Player chooses 1 free upgrade, then can spend Energy on shop items
- Creates decision: "Save Energy for next level vs. buy item now"

REROLL COST:
   RerollCost(n) = 10 × (1.5)^n

   First reroll: 10 Energy (~20% of level-up cost)
   Second: 15 Energy
   Third: 23 Energy
   Fourth: 34 Energy (gets expensive fast)

RATIONALE:
- Shop items cost roughly 1-2 level-ups worth of Energy
- Affordable but requires planning
- Rerolls are cheap initially (encourage experimentation)
- Expensive rerolls prevent reroll-spam for perfect items


V. UPGRADE POWER SCALING

A. DAMAGE UPGRADES

CURRENT PROBLEM: +1/+2/+3/+5 feels arbitrary and weak

NEW FORMULA:
   DamageBonus(rarity) = BaseDamage × Multiplier[rarity]

   Common:    +25% (1.0 → 1.25)
   Uncommon:  +35% (1.0 → 1.35)  
   Rare:      +50% (1.0 → 1.5)
   Legendary: +100% (1.0 → 2.0)

STACKING: Multiplicative with diminishing returns
   2 Common upgrades: 1.0 × 1.25 × 1.20 = 1.5× damage

WHY MULTIPLICATIVE:
- Scales with base damage
- Each upgrade remains valuable late game
- Creates exponential power curve that matches enemy scaling
- Percentage is intuitive ("50% more damage!")

ALTERNATIVE FOR CLARITY: Use additive flat bonuses that scale with level
   Common: +2 damage per upgrade level
   Uncommon: +3 damage per upgrade level
   Rare: +5 damage per upgrade level
   Legendary: +8 damage per upgrade level

   At Level 1: Common gives +2 (double starter damage of 1)
   At Level 10: Common gives +20 (significant chunk)

This keeps flat numbers but makes them meaningful at all stages.


B. FIRE RATE UPGRADES

CURRENT: Reduces time between shots (milliseconds are confusing)

NEW: Express as "Shots per Second"
   Base: 2.0 shots/sec (500ms between shots)
   Common: +0.5 shots/sec
   Uncommon: +0.8 shots/sec
   Rare: +1.2 shots/sec
   Legendary: +2.0 shots/sec

CONVERSION: 
   FireRateMS = 1000 / ShotsPerSecond

DISPLAY: "Fire Rate: 2.5 shots/sec" (clear and intuitive)


C. HEALTH UPGRADES

CURRENT: Flat +20/+30/+40/+50 additions

PROBLEM: Early game +20 is huge (20% of 100), late game +20 is tiny

NEW FORMULA:
   HealthBonus(rarity, currentMax) = currentMax × Multiplier[rarity]

   Common: +15% max health
   Uncommon: +20% max health
   Rare: +30% max health
   Legendary: +50% max health

EXAMPLE:
   Start: 100 HP
   Common upgrade: 115 HP
   Another Common: 132 HP
   Rare upgrade: 172 HP

Always meaningful relative to current power level.


D. UTILITY UPGRADES

PERCENTAGES FOR PROBABILITIES:
   Critical Chance: +3% / +5% / +8% / +12%
   Lifesteal: +1% / +2% / +3% / +5%
   Pickup Chance: +5% / +8% / +12% / +18%

MULTIPLIERS FOR MODIFIERS:
   Critical Damage: +25% / +40% / +60% / +100%
   Movement Speed: +10% / +15% / +25% / +40%

FLAT BONUSES FOR COUNTS:
   Multishot: +1 projectile (all rarities, but legendary grants +2)
   Pierce: +1 pierce (all rarities)
   Chain: +1 chain (all rarities)


VI. DIFFICULTY SCALING & PACING

A. WAVE SYSTEM

Instead of continuous spawning, implement WAVES with breaks:

WAVE STRUCTURE:
   Combat Phase (30-45 seconds)
   - Enemies spawn at regular intervals
   - Intensity builds (spawn rate increases slightly)
   - Energy collection phase

   Consolidation Phase (5-8 seconds)
   - No new spawns
   - Clear remaining enemies
   - Collect drops, breathe, prepare

   Level Up / Shop (20 seconds)
   - Choose upgrade
   - Browse shop
   - Strategic decisions without pressure

WAVE SCALING:
   WaveDuration = 30 + (WaveNumber × 2)

   Wave 1: 30 seconds
   Wave 5: 38 seconds  
   Wave 10: 50 seconds
   Wave 15: 60 seconds (cap)

This creates natural rhythm and prevents fatigue.


B. BOSS ENCOUNTERS

Every 5 levels, spawn a MINI-BOSS:

STATS:
   HP: Sum of 10 normal enemies of current level
   Damage: 1.5× normal enemy damage
   Size: 2× normal enemy size
   Energy Drop: 50 + (Level × 5)

   Level 5 Boss: 190 HP, drops 75 Energy
   Level 10 Boss: 210 HP, drops 100 Energy

PATTERNS:
   - Bullet hell pattern (spray of projectiles)
   - Movement pattern (doesn't just move down)
   - Visual distinction (different color, glowing)

PURPOSE:
   - Milestone moments that feel epic
   - Large energy payoff rewards good play
   - Tests player's build against significant challenge


VII. BALANCING NEW CONTENT

A. ITEM BUDGET SYSTEM

When adding new shop items, use this formula:

ITEM VALUE POINTS = (Cost in Energy) / 10

Example: Tier 2 item costs 137 Energy = 13.7 value points

STAT COSTS (value points per stat benefit):
   +10% Damage: 2.0 points
   +5% Fire Rate: 1.5 points
   +10% Max Health: 1.5 points
   +1% Critical Chance: 1.0 point
   +5% Critical Damage: 0.8 points
   +1% Lifesteal: 1.2 points
   +10% Movement Speed: 1.0 point
   +1 Multishot: 3.0 points
   +1 Pierce: 2.5 points
   +1 Chain: 2.5 points
   Special Mechanic: 4.0-8.0 points (designer discretion)

EXAMPLE ITEM: "Flank Cannons" (Tier 2, 137 Energy)
   Budget: 13.7 points

   Allocation:
   - Enables side-shot mechanic: 5.0 points
   - +15% Fire Rate on side shots: 2.25 points
   - +10% Damage on side shots: 2.0 points
   - Fires during charge: 4.0 points (unique defensive value)

   Total: 13.25 points (within budget)


B. UPGRADE BUDGET SYSTEM

For procedural/custom upgrades:

UPGRADE VALUE = RarityMultiplier × StatValue

RARITY MULTIPLIERS:
   Common: 1.0
   Uncommon: 1.5
   Rare: 2.2
   Legendary: 3.5

EXAMPLE: Damage Upgrade
   Common: +25% damage = 1.0 × 2.5 = 2.5 value
   Legendary: +100% damage = 3.5 × 2.5 = 8.75 value

This ensures legendary upgrades are actually ~3.5× as impactful as common.

IX. TESTING & VALIDATION

A. KEY METRICS

Track these across playtests:

1. TIME TO LEVEL
   - Target: 25-40 seconds average across Levels 1-10
   - If too fast: Reduce energy drops or increase requirements
   - If too slow: Increase drops or reduce requirements

2. ITEMS PURCHASED PER RUN
   - Target: 3-5 items in a 15-minute run
   - If too few: Reduce item costs or increase drops
   - If too many: Increase costs

3. DAMAGE REQUIRED TO KILL
   - Basic Enemy: Should take 3-8 shots throughout game
   - Tank Enemy: Should take 8-15 shots throughout game
   - If enemies feel tanky: Increase damage upgrades
   - If enemies melt: Increase HP scaling

4. DEATHS PER RUN
   - Target: 0-1 deaths per run for average player
   - If too many: Reduce enemy damage or improve telegraphing
   - If none: Increase difficulty

5. PLAYER POWER FEELING
   - Survey question: "Did you feel noticeably stronger at Level 10 vs Level 1?"
   - Target: 90%+ say "Yes, significantly"


B. PLAYTEST SCENARIOS

Run these specific scenarios to validate balance:

SCENARIO 1: Pure Damage Build
   - Take only damage upgrades, no shop items
   - Should clear Level 10 in ~8 minutes
   - Should feel powerful but not invincible

SCENARIO 2: Defensive Build
   - Take health, lifesteal, and defensive items
   - Should survive longer but clear slower
   - Should feel tanky and strategic

SCENARIO 3: Fire Rate Build
   - Stack fire rate and multishot upgrades
   - Should have screen full of bullets
   - Should feel chaotic but effective

SCENARIO 4: Economic Play
   - Buy maximum shop items, minimal upgrades
   - Should feel versatile with unique mechanics
   - Should be competitive with pure upgrade paths

All scenarios should feel viable and reach Level 10+.


X. ADVANCED CONSIDERATIONS

A. SOFT CAPS & DIMINISHING RETURNS

For stats that can stack infinitely:

   Formula: EffectiveValue = SoftCap × (1 - e^(-Value/SoftCap))

   Critical Chance soft cap at 50%
   - At 25%: Effective 22% (full value)
   - At 50%: Effective 32% (diminishing)
   - At 100%: Effective 43% (heavily diminished)

This prevents degenerate "100% crit" builds while keeping investment worthwhile.


B. SYNERGY BONUSES

When player has related upgrades/items:

EXAMPLE: Chain + Multishot
   - Normally: Chain hits 1 target per bounce
   - Synergy: Chain hits 2 targets per bounce
   - Bonus: +15% chain damage

Displayed in UI: "Synergy Detected: Chain Lightning"

This rewards build diversity and experimentation.


C. RISK/REWARD MECHANICS

CURSED ITEMS (Optional Advanced Feature):
   - Shop offers "Cursed" items at 50% cost
   - Powerful effect + negative effect
   - Example: "Cursed Targeting Suite"
      - +150% Critical Damage
      - -25% Base Damage

Creates interesting decisions for advanced players.
